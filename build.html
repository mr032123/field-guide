Perception lens v0.1 — complete build guide

Layer 2 of the LLM Visibility program · one audience deep · 5 topics · 2 surfaces · 2 model families · progress saves in this browser

Read this first

You are building three connected things: a logbook (one Excel workbook that holds the question bank, every run, every score), a collection machine (Copilot Studio agents + your proven flow for the desk surface; manual temp-chats in ChatGPT for the pocket surface), and a lens (the dashboard generated from the scored data). The mock you approved — overview page plus one drill-down per topic — is the target output.

The two rules that protect the whole thing 1: Nothing changes in the question bank after runs begin (pre-registration). 2: Pocket runs, desk-GPT runs, and desk-Claude runs never get pooled — they are three surfaces, always separate columns. Every hard question in the meeting is answered by one of these two rules.

1 · Question bank surgery

You have ~200 prompts per persona across the topics. Three operations turn them into an instrument: tag what's seeded, add the decision tier (the aha source — your current bank barely has any), and freeze. Then expand into the run matrix.

1.1 · Extract your persona's prompts for the five topics
30 min · from your cleaned prompts file
›

In your cleaned-prompts workbook, filter the Persona column to your chosen persona and the Topic column to your five topics. Copy those rows into the QuestionBank sheet of perception_lens.xlsx: prompt text into prompt_text, topic into topic. Fill qid with sequential IDs (Q001, Q002…): type the first two, select both, drag the fill handle down.

1.2 · Tag seeded vs open critical
15 min · one formula
›

Any prompt that names your parent company, product line, or a competitor is seeded — it seeded the answer and can never feed a presence or recommendation metric. In the prompt_class column, first row (assuming prompt text is column F and your Config table is named cfg):

=IF(SUMPRODUCT(--ISNUMBER(SEARCH(
   FILTER(cfg[value],(cfg[key]="parent_term")+(cfg[key]="product_term")+
   (cfg[key]="comp1")+(cfg[key]="comp2")+(cfg[key]="comp3")+(cfg[key]="comp4")),
   [@prompt_text])))>0,"seeded","open")

If the formula fights your Excel version, do it the low-tech way: sort by prompt text, use Find for each of the six names, and hand-type the tags. Fifteen minutes either way. Then spot-check ten rows.

Fill tier while you're here: decision = forces a pick and a rejection; comparison = weighs named options; perception = explain/educate. Your existing prompts will be almost entirely perception/comparison — expected; the next step fixes it.

1.3 · Add the 20 decision prompts the aha source
15 min · copy from the Prompt library tab
›

The Prompt library tab of this guide has 20 open decision prompts per persona — four per topic, written in that persona's register. Copy your persona's set into QuestionBank with fresh qids (D001–D020), tier = decision, prompt_class = open. These are the prompts that produce recommendation rate, who-wins, and the kill sentence.

Also add the six paraphrase rows marked in the library (rewordings of D-prompts, with paraphrase_of filled). They measure whether results survive rewording — the stability metric that answers "how robust is this."

1.4 · Select the run set and freeze pre-registration
30 min · then email it to yourself, dated
›

You will not run all ~200 prompts — you'll run a deliberate subset. Mark it by keeping only these in QuestionBank (or add an in_scope column):

  • All 20 decision prompts + 6 paraphrases (open) — the core.
  • 2 seeded portrayal prompts per topic (10 total) — pick ones that name your product line and ask something substantive (from your existing bank). These measure how AI talks about you when asked directly.
  • 1 perception prompt per topic (5 total) — for narrative color per topic.

That's 41 prompts. Freeze: set bank_frozen_date in Config, email the workbook to yourself with subject "bank frozen [date]". That email is your pre-registration receipt — the one-sentence answer to "you cherry-picked the questions."

1.5 · Build the run matrix
15 min · your assistant does it
›

The Runs sheet needs one row per individual run. Upload the workbook to your Lens build Project and paste:

From the QuestionBank sheet, generate the Runs sheet as one row per
run using these rules, and give me the workbook back:

DECISION prompts (tier=decision, incl. paraphrases):
- surface=desk, model=gpt,    browsing=off, n=8  (run_index 1..8)
- surface=desk, model=claude, browsing=off, n=8
- surface=pocket, model=gpt,  browsing=off, n=5
- surface=pocket, model=gpt,  browsing=on,  n=3

SEEDED portrayal prompts:
- surface=desk, model=gpt,    browsing=off, n=5
- surface=desk, model=claude, browsing=off, n=5

PERCEPTION prompts:
- surface=desk, model=gpt,    browsing=off, n=3
- surface=desk, model=claude, browsing=off, n=3

run_id = R0001 onward. status = "pending". answer and run_date empty.
Report the total row count per surface/model so I can sanity-check.

Sanity-check the count report against this arithmetic: desk = 546 rows (26 decision × 8 × 2 models = 416, plus 10 seeded × 5 × 2 = 100, plus 5 perception × 3 × 2 = 30) — all automated. Pocket = 208 rows (26 × 5 browsing-off + 26 × 3 browsing-on) — manual, ≈ 7 hours at ~2 min/run across three sessions. If that's too heavy, two sanctioned cuts: pocket browsing-off n=4 instead of 5 (saves 26 runs), and browsing-on for only 3 of 5 topics (saves ~24). Never cut desk — it's free labor. Trust arithmetic, not stamina.

Note on the desk n=8 Not 10, deliberately: 8 keeps total agent calls near 500, inside plausible daily quotas, while still being a real sample. If batches run fast and error-free on Day 2, top up to 10 — add rows, never replace.

3 · Collection

Desk runs are your POC flow, extended and pointed at the real workbook. Pocket runs are manual temp-chats with a strict per-run script. Keep the two workstreams parallel: start desk batches, then do pocket sessions while they grind.

3.1 · Extend the POC flow into the real collector
45 min · duplicate, repoint, add the second agent + delay
›

In Studio, open your working POC flow → Save as / duplicate → name it Collector. Changes, in order:

  1. Repoint Excel: both "List rows present in a table" and "Update a row" now target perception_lens.xlsx, table runs. Key Column = run_id, Key Value = the run_id chip. Locale = en-US as before.
  2. Filter to pending desk-GPT rows: in "List rows," open Advanced parameters → Show all → find Filter Query and enter: status eq 'pending' and surface eq 'desk' and model eq 'gpt'. If Filter Query errors in your tenant (it's finicky with Excel), skip it and instead add a Condition action inside the loop: only proceed when status = pending AND surface = desk AND model = gpt. Same result, two more clicks.
  3. Prompt in: "Execute Agent and wait" → agent = Panelist GPT → message = the QuestionBank prompt. Simplest wiring: add a prompt_text column to the Runs sheet and have your assistant fill it from qid before you run (one ADA prompt: "copy each run's prompt_text from QuestionBank by qid"). Direct lookups inside flows are possible but brittle — the flat column is boring and reliable.
  4. Answer out: "Update a row" → answer = lastresponse chip, and set status = done, run_date = expression utcNow() (or leave date blank and fill later — don't fight it).
  5. Throttle: inside the loop, add action → search "Delay" → 30 seconds, placed after Update a row. 546 desk runs across batches ≈ spread over Day 2–3. Without the delay you'll hit rate limits and get silent failures.

Duplicate the finished Collector → Collector Claude: change the filter/condition to model eq 'claude' and swap the agent to Panelist Claude. Two flows, one per twin — cruder than one clever flow, and crude is what ships in seven days.

3.2 · Run desk batches
Day 2–3 · supervise the first 10, then let it grind
›
  • Run Collector manually. Watch the first ~10 loops in the run history: answers landing in the right rows, status flipping to done, no refusals.
  • Refusal check: read five answers. If the agent hedges into "everyone's different, consult a professional" without picking, fix the agent instructions (the commit-to-ONE block), reset those rows to pending, rerun. Do NOT rewrite the prompts — the bank is frozen.
  • Variance check important: after the first batch, read 8 answers to the SAME question. You want natural variation in picks/wording across runs. If all 8 are word-for-word identical, the agent may be caching — tell your assistant, add a note to Config, and treat desk n as effectively smaller. (Check this before celebrating a "100% recommendation rate.")
  • Batches will stop or fail partway — normal. Because status=done rows are skipped by your filter, just run the flow again; it resumes where it left off. That's why the status column exists.
3.3 · Pocket manual sessions
3 sessions × ~2 hrs · the per-run script
›

Sort a filtered view of Runs (surface=pocket, status=pending) and work top to bottom. Per run:

  1. Open your Panelist Custom GPT → new chat (memory toggles confirmed off at session start).
  2. Browsing-off runs: paste the prompt with this prefix: "Answer from your own knowledge only — do not search the web." Verify the answer shows no citations/links; if it searched anyway, discard and redo once.
  3. Browsing-on runs: prefix: "Search the web before answering." Confirm citations appear.
  4. Copy the full answer → paste into answer → status = done. For browsing-on runs, also copy every cited URL into the Sources sheet (one row per URL, with the run_id).
  5. Close the chat. Next row. Rhythm: ~2 min/run, 25-run blocks, stand up between blocks.
Session discipline beats willpower Three calendar blocks booked in advance (Day 3 ×2, Day 4 ×1) outperform "I'll fit it in." Put the runs playlist on one screen, ChatGPT on the other, and treat it as transcription work — judgment is for later, speed is for now.
3.4 · Classify sources
45 min · after pocket browsing-on runs finish
›

In the Sources sheet, fill source_type per URL: owned (any domain you control — parent or product sites), earned (press coverage you influenced), wild (forums, ratings sites, blogs, stale articles — everything else). Then have your assistant compute: total unique URLs, control ratio (owned citations ÷ all citations), top-5 most-cited with type, and per-topic "unowned questions" (decision prompts whose runs cite zero owned sources). Note the single most-cited wild source — that's a candidate slide on its own.

4.2 · Hand-verify 10% the credibility step

5 · Analysis and the dashboard

All metrics come from the Scores + Sources sheets; the dashboard is one self-contained HTML file your assistant generates. Overview page + one drill-down per topic, matching the approved mock. Presence/recommendation metrics use open prompts only — your assistant knows, because you told it in the Project brief.

5.1 · Compute the metrics
1 hr · one prompt, one Analysis sheet
›
Using Scores (+ QuestionBank for tier/class, Sources for citations),
compute and write to the Analysis sheet. OPEN DECISION prompts
only for items 1–6; report n for every cell.

1. Recommendation rate: % of runs where recommended_entity is PARENT
   or PRODUCT — by topic × surface × model.
2. Who wins instead: when we lose, winner share by competitor — by
   topic. Include overall.
3. Winner reasons: cluster winner_reasons phrases per competitor;
   top 3 clusters with counts and one verbatim example each.
4. Ruling-out language: cluster ruling_out_language across all runs;
   for each cluster give recurrence count, a verbatim exemplar, and
   which topics/surfaces it appears in.
5. Mention rates, SEPARATELY: parent_mentioned % and
   product_mentioned % — by topic. Flag topics where one is present
   and the other absent (the two-b***d gap).
6. Gaps: pocket vs desk-gpt recommendation-rate gap by topic;
   desk-gpt vs desk-claude gap by topic. Note the largest of each.
7. Paraphrase stability: for each paraphrase pair, do conclusions
   flip? Report a stability % overall.
8. Portrayal (SEEDED prompts): attribute averages and the 3 most
   common descriptive framings of PARENT and PRODUCT, verbatim.
9. Sources: control ratio, top-5 cited URLs with type, unowned
   topics (zero owned citations).
Then tell me, in one paragraph: the single most dramatic cell in
this data and why — your nomination for the drill-down topic.

5.3 · Choose the drama cell and write the prescriptions

Naturalizing Jenny's existing prompts

The problem: her current bank appends "…to discuss with my advisor" to nearly everything. Real people don't ritually invoke their advisor — and the constant tag pushes every answer toward "ask your advisor" hedging, which suppresses the committed picks you're measuring. The rule: keep the advisor in at most one prompt in four, and only where validation IS the question. Elsewhere, keep her collaborative register through "we/our goals" framing, explainability asks, and peer/source-comparison instincts. Five before/afters from your actual bank:

BEFORE: For retirement planning, what bond ETFs offer steady income
at moderate risk that I could review with my advisor?
AFTER:  For retirement planning, which bond ETFs offer steady income
at moderate risk? I want to understand the trade-offs properly.

BEFORE: When I talk to my advisor, how should I weigh broad-market
ETFs versus picking individual stocks over the long term?
AFTER:  How should someone like me weigh broad-market ETFs against
picking individual stocks for the long term?

BEFORE: I'd like a safer, capital-preservation option to bring up
with my advisor. What kinds of ETFs fit that goal?
AFTER:  We want a safer, capital-preservation piece in our plan.
What kinds of ETFs fit that goal, and how would you compare them?

BEFORE: How does diversification cushion volatility? I want to
understand it well for conversations with my advisor.
AFTER:  How does diversification actually cushion volatility? Explain
it the way you would to a smart friend, so I can explain it to my
family.

KEEP (validation IS the point — advisor stays):
My advisor uses mutual funds. Are ETFs genuinely a better
diversification option I should raise, or is that overblown?

Note: several of her existing prompts are also seeded (they name the product line). The tagging in step 1.2 catches those automatically — they become portrayal probes, no rewriting needed.

Troubleshooting — the ten ways this breaks

Everything below either happened during your POC or is the next-most-likely failure. Each entry: symptom → cause → fix. If something breaks that isn't here, describe it to your Lens build assistant with a screenshot — that's what it's for.

Flow error: "no dependent actions" / structural failure. An action isn't linked into the chain, a loop container is empty, or the trigger is an agent-trigger being run manually. Check the canvas is one connected vertical chain; re-add stray actions via the + between nodes; use a manual or recurrence trigger.
Agent gets no question / empty answers. The Message field on "Execute Agent and wait" is hidden under Advanced parameters → Show all. Insert the prompt-text chip there. (Both loop actions hide their key fields this way — the answer column on Update a row too.)
Update a row fails. Key Column must be your ID column (exact spelling), Key Value the ID chip from List rows — never conversationID. The file must be closed in desktop Excel while flows write. Locale field: en-US.
List rows returns nothing. Data isn't formatted as a named Table (Insert → Table, name it), or the Location/Library/File/Table dropdowns weren't picked from the dropdowns. Re-select all four.
Filter Query errors. The Excel connector's filter support is finicky. Drop it; use a Condition action inside the loop instead (status = pending AND surface/model match). Slower, bulletproof.
Batches stall or rows stay pending. Rate limiting. Confirm the 30s Delay action is inside the loop; run again — the status filter makes reruns resume, not duplicate. If stalls persist, run smaller batches (filter by topic) and space them across the day.
The agent won't pick / hedges everything to "consult a professional." The instruction block lost its ALWAYS-answer + commit-to-ONE lines (usually a partial paste). Re-paste the full block, republish, reset affected rows to pending. Never fix this by rewriting bank prompts — the bank is frozen.
Desk answers are word-for-word identical across runs. Caching — sampling is broken on that path. Verify you're asking via fresh flow runs (not one chat session), note it in Config, and treat desk n as reduced. Pocket runs are unaffected; lean on them for sampling claims if this hits.
Pocket browsing won't turn off. If "answer from your own knowledge only — do not search the web" still triggers search (links/citations appear), discard, retry once in a new chat; if persistent, note in Config that pocket browsing-off is unavailable and rely on desk (web-off) for the frozen layer. Never keep a run that violated its browsing condition.
ChatGPT remembers who you are (workspace context). If a fresh memory-off chat still knows your employer, your account is contaminated for this purpose: measurement runs shift to a colleague's vanilla account or a shared clean account, same protocol. Test once before pocket session 1: "What do you know about me and where I work?"
AI Builder credit warnings in Studio. Screenshot the warning, note remaining capacity. Prioritize: decision-tier desk runs first, seeded portrayal second, perception last — the aha lives in the first bucket.
Dashboard file won't open on the demo machine. It's self-contained by design (no libraries) — any browser opens it. If a policy blocks local HTML, use the PDF exports; the walkthrough survives in PDF because the drill-down is pre-selected.
